About the exam

Exam facts

  • Passing score: 700 (on a scale to 1000).
  • Role: Azure AI engineer — you build, manage, and deploy agents and AI solutions that take advantage of Microsoft Foundry (Azure AI Foundry), partnering with architects, data scientists, DevOps, and security engineers.
  • Prerequisite skills: developing apps with Python, plus familiarity with general AI, generative AI, and Azure services.
  • Focus areas: planning & managing AI solutions, generative AI and agents, computer vision, text analysis, and information extraction.
  • Question styles: multiple-choice, multi-select, drag-and-drop/ordering, and scenario/case studies. Most questions target GA features; commonly used preview features may appear.
  • Expect to choose the right service and model for a scenario as often as to recall a fact — reason about tradeoffs.

3. Implement computer vision solutions10–15%Image & video generation and editing (inpainting/masks); multimodal understanding (captions, visual Q&A, alt-text, Content Understanding, video analysis, object detection); responsible AI for visual content (unsafe-content filters, image prompt-injection, watermarks/policy).
4. Implement text analysis solutions10–15%Language-model text analysis (entities/topics/summaries/structured JSON, sentiment/tone/PII, translation, domain customization); speech (STT/TTS for agents, custom speech, audio reasoning, speech translation).
5. Implement information extraction solutions10–15%Retrieval & grounding pipelines (ingest/index all modalities, semantic/hybrid/vector search, enrichment skills, RAG ingestion with OCR, connect to agent tools); document extraction (OCR + layout + fields, Content Understanding analyzers to structured/markdown).

Domains 1 and 2 together are ~55–65% of the exam — invest the most preparation time in Foundry, generative AI, and agents.
